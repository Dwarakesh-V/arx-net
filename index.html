This section provides guidance on how to use the Arx Net application effectively. To learn more about how the underlying application operates, please refer the GitHub

Graph Generation

  • Graph names must be unique.
  • Edges must be declared in one of the following formats:
    • (A,B,5) — Edge from vertex A to B with a weight of 5
    • AB-4 — Edge from vertex A to B with a weight of -4
    • 12-6 — Edge from vertex 1 to 2 with a weight of -6
    Edges can be directed or undirected, and weighted or unweighted. These states are dynamically evaluated based on your active checkbox selections.
  • Click 'Generate Graph' to compile the inputted topology.
  • Alternatively, click 'Generate random graph/tree' to procedurally build a network based on your configuration parameters:
    • Settings within this section restrict the procedural generation. For instance, self-loops are explicitly disallowed unless the corresponding checkbox is activated.
    • The weighted and directed checkboxes dictate the structural properties of the resulting procedural topology.

Outliner Controls

  • The outliner displays a hierarchical list of all generated graphs, offering global toggles to delete or hide all structures simultaneously.
  • Double-click on a graph's name to rename it. (Remember: All names must remain unique.)
  • Click the 'Focus' button to immediately center the associated graph window, bringing it to the visual foreground and highlighting its active state.
  • Right-click on a graph name to access contextual actions (Delete / Duplicate):
    • Warning: Deleting a graph is a destructive action and cannot be undone.
    • Duplicating a graph allows you to alter its fundamental properties by toggling the directed and weighted checkboxes prior to executing the duplication.

Graph Window Controls

  • Use the Left Mouse Button (LMB) to select and interact directly with vertices and edges.
  • Use the Middle Mouse Button, Ctrl + LMB, or double-click & drag to pan across the viewport canvas.
  • Use the Scroll Wheel (or trackpad pinch) to smoothly zoom in and out of the active topology.
  • Right-click anywhere within the window background to summon a contextual menu containing advanced viewport interactions.
  • Double-click on an empty area of the window to automatically reset and re-center the graph relative to your current camera view.
  • Click the Book Icon to toggle the algorithm results panel. Every computed algorithm features an interactive [Explanation] link that dynamically maps out the step-by-step logic in the right-hand panel.
  • The Force checkbox activates a physics-based layout engine. This applies simulated repulsions and attractions to the nodes collectively, preventing visual overlap or excessive spacing while dragging.
